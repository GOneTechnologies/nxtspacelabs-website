1. What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small text file that a website places on your device to remember something about your visit. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, and pixel tags. We refer to all of these collectively as "cookies" in this policy.

2. Categories we use

We group cookies into three simple categories:

  • Strictly necessary — the site does not work without them. These handle authentication, security, and basic session state.
  • Preferences — remember choices you have made (e.g. sound-on, day/night look, language).
  • Analytics — help us understand which pages are being used, and where the site is broken. Never used to build advertising profiles.

4. Our products may set additional cookies

If you use PairNow or another one of our products, that product may set its own cookies for its own operation. Each product's cookies are documented within the product's own settings and privacy pages.

5. Choices

You can accept, decline, or delete cookies at any time:

  • Browser settings — most browsers let you block cookies globally or on a per-site basis.
  • Preferences — declining preference or analytics cookies will not break the site.
  • Strictly necessary cookies cannot be turned off without breaking sign-in and other core features.

6. Third parties

Where we use third parties (like Google Fonts or an analytics provider), those third parties act as processors under our written contracts. We do not sell or share your data for advertising purposes.

7. Changes to this policy

When we add or remove a cookie, we update this page and refresh the "last updated" date at the top.
